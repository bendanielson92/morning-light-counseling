# Editing Your Website — Quick Guide for Billy

Your website has a built-in control panel. You can change nearly every word on the site yourself, from any computer, without touching code.

## Getting in

1. Go to **your-site.com/keystatic**
2. Log in with your GitHub account (Ben set this up for you)

## What you can edit

| In the panel | What it changes |
|---|---|
| **Site Settings** | Phone, email, address, office hours, insurance list, self-pay rate, the client portal link, and the license line in the footer |
| **Homepage** | Every headline and paragraph on the front page |
| **Counseling Pages** | The six pages (Anxiety, Depression, Anger, Habits, Marriage, Life Transitions) — including each page's FAQs. You can add new pages here too; they appear on the site automatically. |
| **Men's Counseling Page** | The long-form page, section by section |
| **About Page** | Your bio, approach, credentials, headshot photo |
| **What to Expect Page** | The three steps, the practical details, and the FAQs |
| **Blog Posts** | Write a post, flip off the "Draft" switch, and the Blog section appears in the site menu automatically. (While there are no published posts, there's no blog link — nothing looks empty.) |

## How saving works

Click **Save** and your change goes live automatically in about a minute. Nothing publishes until you hit Save, so you can't break anything by looking around.

## Photos

On the About page you can upload a new headshot right in the panel. When you get your office photos taken, send them to Ben and he'll swap the placeholders (or walk you through it — it's the same upload button).

## Three things to know

1. **The form on the Contact page emails you directly.** Reply to inquiries from your regular email — the visitor's address is set as the reply-to.
2. **Don't paste text from Word with weird formatting** — type into the panel or paste as plain text.
3. **If something looks wrong after a save**, don't panic: every save is stored in history and Ben can roll back any change.
